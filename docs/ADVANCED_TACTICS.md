## 1. Points of Difference (POD) Strategy

### What is a POD?
A Point of Difference is a player owned by less than 15% of coaches who outscores their ownership percentage would suggest.

### POD Value Formula
```
POD Value = (Actual Score - Expected Score) × (100 - Ownership%)

Where Expected Score = League Average at position
```

### Finding PODs

**Early Season PODs (Rounds 1-6):**
- Underpriced returnees from injury
- Role changes not yet reflected in ownership
- Overlooked mid-pricers

**Mid-Season PODs (Rounds 7-14):**
- Breakout performers not yet mainstream
- Players returning from suspension
- Form reversals

**Late Season PODs (Rounds 15+):**
- Fixture-based targets
- Players with soft run home
- Underowned premiums

## 3. Captaincy Optimization

### Expected Value Captaincy

**Don't just pick highest average - consider:**
```
Captain EV = Projected Score × Confidence Level

Example:
- Bontempelli: 127 avg × 0.95 confidence = 120.6 EV
- Risky forward: 115 avg × 0.70 confidence = 80.5 EV

Pick Bontempelli even though the forward might outscore on their day
```

### Matchup-Based Captaincy
| Opponent Type | Captain Choice |
|---------------|----------------|
| Bottom 4 team | Back ceiling players |
| Top 4 team | Back floor players |
| Wet weather | Back inside mids |
| MCG/Marvel | Back accumulators |

### Captain Rotation Strategy
Maintain 3-4 captain options across different teams to handle:
- Byes
- Injuries
- Fixture swings
- Loophole opportunities

## 4. Structure Optimization

### The 6-8-2-6 vs 5-9-2-6 Debate

**6 Defenders:**
- Safer, more depth
- Harder to find 6 elite options
- Best for: Conservative coaches

**5 Defenders:**
- More midfield firepower
- Requires perfect injury luck
- Best for: Aggressive coaches

### Bench Structure

**Option A: Playing Rookies**
- All bench spots are cash cows
- Maximum cash generation
- Risk: Low emergency scores

**Option B: Premium Emergency**
- 1-2 bench spots are mid-priced
- Better emergency coverage
- Trade-off: Slower cash generation

### Recommended 2026 Structure
```
DEF: 3 Premium + 2 Value + 1 Rookie
MID: 4 Premium + 2 Value + 2 Rookies
RUC: 1 Premium + 1 Rookie
FWD: 2 Premium + 2 Value + 2 Rookies
BENCH: 4 Playing Rookies
```

## 6. Bye Round Mastery

### The Perfect Bye Structure

**Pre-Bye Target:**
- Maximum 5 players on any single bye
- Minimum 18 playing players each bye round
- DPPs positioned for flexibility

### Bye Trading Strategy

**2 Weeks Before Bye:**
- Identify weakest bye round
- Target players from opposite bye

**Bye Week:**
- Don't burn trades just for bye cover
- Accept some point loss if trade value poor

**Week After Bye:**
- Resume normal trading

## 8. Risk Management

### Portfolio Theory for SuperCoach

**Diversify:**
- No more than 3 players from one team
- Balance ceiling and floor players
- Mix young and veteran

**Correlation Risk:**
- Bulldogs players all on same bye
- Teammates competing for same ball
- All premiums from same position

### The "Anti-Template" Strategy

When 70%+ coaches have same player:
- If they fail, you're equal
- If you have a POD who beats them, you gain

**Strategic contrarianism pays in overall rankings**
